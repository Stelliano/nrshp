SACOS History

Why the need for the NRSHP?

The NRSHP celebrates its 10th Anniversary in June 2024. We have much to celebrate, given what we have achieved thus far. The NRSHP was formed to record the proud and rich history of non-racial in the old Transvaal (now Gauteng) or the codes/members or as most would know TRACOS the Transvaal Council on Sport (TRACOS). There is no doubt that the history of non-racial sport has been neglected. In many ways our history has been suppressed by the now establishment sport and those that govern nationally.

This neglect has unfortunately created a perception in most previously white based sporting codes that we (black people) never played sport. It is also disappointing that, that perception is held by many journalists and administrators of sport today. It is of even more concern that some of the leaders of these organisations came from the ranks of the oppressed disenfranchised sports organisations or non-racial sport. They keep quite about the history because they are embarrassed about the failure and total destruction of community sport as we know it. The unifications that took place was a total sellout of grassroots non racial sport. The school and sport policies that evolved further destroyed the very basis of community or grassroots sport.

SACOS 50th History Colloquium held at Wits 28-29 July 2023

The launch of this additional platform or portal will simply serve to ensure that we inform all society of the fact that we (black people) played non-racial sport, not multi-racial or multi-national sport. SACOS was the foremost co-ordinating sports organisation that was non-partisan fighting for the expulsion of the racist sport internationally. SACOS supported all the liberation movements and received the support from all of them up to the transition in the late 1980’s and early 1990’s when the ruling party decided to sell out the most fundamental basis of developing sport.

Their actions is unquestionably the reason why there are no or very little sports structures in the previous vibrant and active coloured/Indian/black communities. TIRFU had 14 clubs and is now one club Raider RFC. Lenasia FA and Bosmont FA were the biggest football associations, now neither enjoys LFA status. Lens hosted the biggest junior cricket league in the province now there is nothing. There were codes like tennis, table tennis, volleyball, baseball, softball clubs, chess clubs, woman and men’s hockey in all our communities. There was 6 darts associations around the Transvaal Province now there is no league darts played in any of our communities in Gauteng. The saddest fact is the destruction of school sport. School sport was the foundation of mass participation in sport and through the various competitions and championships allowed elite athletes to rise to the top and compete at a regional, provincial and national level.

If one scans the history content of any SASCOC or Gauteng Sports Council’s websites and their codes of sports, you will find very little or no history of the TRACOS, SACOS and its members history. SACOS and all its members have a rich history of not only having great athletes in, clubs regions, provinces and at national level.

The historic SACOS Colloquium hosted by the NRSHP RSC at Wits in July 2023 clearly detailed how non-racial sport was destroyed by the unity talks and the architects of the policies all points to the ruling party and their comrades deployed to destroy SACOS. No Normal Sport in an Abnormal Society. Sport could have played a unifying and positive tool and vehicle for change. They must accept responsibility for their actions.

With you and these platforms we hope to change that perception and record the real and factual rich history, highs, lows and struggles of non-racial sportspeople. We will tell the true history of non-racial sport not only provincially but nationally too.
